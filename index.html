Webdeveloper, Photojournalist & Thinker.

After studying Sociology & History, at University of Potsdam, I was searching for a language, to express all those thoughts that I had in mind about concepts analysing behavoir or people. I found a possibility in telling stories of everyday life, which are bigger than the particular situation. Later I studied Photojournalism & Documentary Photography in the class of Rolf Nobel & Lars Bauernschmitt at the University of Applied Science and Arts in Hannover. After long nights drinking schnapps, we transformed the existing collective visual-rebellion, into a multimedia-collective, dedicated to progressive storytelling, called jib-collective. In June 2015 we published our fist common story "The Silence of the Lambs". My current photographic work deals with the the complex of racism, migration and social struggles.

In parallel, the small team of WIENFLUSS, one of the leading agencies in creating an accessible web in Austria, and Me drafted and implemented many websites, such as wien.info (official Vienna Tourism Website), Bundeskanzleramt (Department of Federal Chancellor Austria), EEA & Enercoach (Assessing Europe's leading energy saving communities) and OeNB (Austrian National Bank). I mainly worked on the User Experience Design, Accessibility and the frontend code.

This experience lead me into several month of freelancing as a Developer. Working in a team with Bildargumente I created a new website for the Alarmphone, helped to create a succesful crowfunding campaign for Sea-Watch 2 and supported some students in Hannover in creating a website for the GAF (gallery for photography).

Well, all this was too much stress and too little teamwork, so I teamed up with the wonderful people of about:source. Together we launched a new website for Pro Asyl in March 2016.

Follow me on jib-collective & github.
