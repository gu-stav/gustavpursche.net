Webdeveloper, Photojournalist & Thinker.

After studying Sociology & History, at University of Potsdam, I was searching for a language, to express all those thoughts that I had in mind about concepts analysing behavoir or people. I found a possibility in telling stories of everyday life, which are bigger than the particular situation. Later I studied Photojournalism & Documentary Photography in the class of Rolf Nobel & Lars Bauernschmitt at the University of Applied Science and Arts in Hannover. After long nights drinking schnapps, we transformed the existing collective visual-rebellion, into a multimedia-collective, dedicated to progressive storytelling, called jib-collective. In June 2015 we published our fist common story "The Silence of the Lambs". My current photographic work deals with the the complex of racism, migration and social struggles.

During my recent job, I consulted and programmed together with a small team at WIENFLUSS many websites, such as wien.info (official Vienna Tourism Website), Bundeskanzleramt (Department of Federal Chancellor Austria), EEA & Enercoach (Assessing Europe's leading energy saving communities) and OeNB (Austrian National Bank). I mainly worked on the User Experience Design, Accessibility and the frontend code.

Follow me on twitter, jib-collective & github.
