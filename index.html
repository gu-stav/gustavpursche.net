Photojournalist, Webdeveloper & Thinker.

I studied Sociology and History at University of Potsdam. While learning about methods of analysing the world I was searching for a language, to express all those thoughts, which I had in mind. The answer I found in telling stories of everyday life, which tell more than just about this particular situation. Later I studied Photojournalism & Documentary Photography in the class of Rolf Nobel & Lars Bauernschmitt at the University of Applied Science and Arts in Hannover. Later I joined the collective visual-rebellion, which we started to transform in 2015 into a multimedia-collective, dedicated to progressive storytelling, called jib-collective. My current photographic work deals with the the complex of racism, migration and social struggles.

During my recent job, I've, together with a small team, consulted and programmed many websites, such as wien.info (official Vienna Tourism Website), Bundeskanzleramt (Department of Federal Chancellor Austria), EEA & Enercoach (Assessing Europe's leading energy saving communities) and OeNB (Austrian National Bank). For those I mainly did Frontend-Development, User Experience Design and Accessibility.

Follow me on twitter, jib-collective, github & visual.rebellion.
